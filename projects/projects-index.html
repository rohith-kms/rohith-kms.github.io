Hardware and Software Development

Team IISc Bangalore: International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition

2017

Represented IISc at the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition. I was part of the hardware team and we developed the Growth Curve and Optical Density device (GCODe), a device to automate the regular measurement of bacterial populations in a culture to generate growth curves. We made two devices - GCODe Mini and Pro. The Pro was still in the development stage at the time of the iGEM conference in Boston, but the Mini was production ready, with a readily available DIY kit and assembly instructions on the website. GCODe was a part of the iGEM IISc 2017 team's larger project, iFLOAT. The project was nominated for the best hardware prize, and won a gold medal at the conference.

aNANt - a functional materials database

2018

aNANt is an ambitious project led by Prof. Abishek Kumar Singh from the Materials Research Centre at IISc. aNANt is a database of over 23,000 computationally generated functional materials and their crystal structure, and the first of its kind in India. It also contains the electronic band structure and properties like lattice constant and magnetic moment for each material. I was the lead developer of the aNANt website, working on both the frontend and the backend, and even setting up the server. This project helped me learn HTML, CSS, Javascript, Django and Apache. The current version of the website was developed independant of me, but you can still find the files for the old version of the website on my GitHub account.
