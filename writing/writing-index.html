Science Communication

To See the World in a Grain of Sand

June 14, 2022

A feature on Tejas G Murthy, Associate Professor at the Department of Civil Engineering at IISc. The article talks about his (awesome) interdisciplinary work on granular materials and ways of analysing them such as Computed Tomography (CT) using powerful X-Ray sources. Published in Kernel Issue 3, 2022.

Using Bacteria to Build Settlements on Mars

June 14, 2022

Extremely cool research by Prof. Aloke Kumar from the Department of Mechanical Engineering at IISc, on using bacteria as cementing agents to form "Space Bricks" from Martian soil, enabling future Martian settlers to build settlements using the soil they find on the planet itself, instead of having to carry it all the way from the Earth. Published in Kernel Issue 3, 2022.

How Randomly Moving Electrons Can Improve Cyber Security

August 12, 2022

A breakthrough device - a True Random Number Generator (TRNG) - invented by Prof. Kausik Majumdar and his team at the Department of Electronics and Communications Engineering at IISc. The TRNG uses the quantum mechanical nature of electrons to generate random numbers, which can pave the way for more secure data encryption. Published in Kernel Issue 4, 2022.

LGBTQIA+ rights and life.

I identify as non-binary, and go by the pronouns They/Them. I have a rich lived experience of being queer in India, and I'm fascinated by the plethora of forms queer life has taken within the fabric of the Indian society.

Why it is Important for Queer People to be Seen in IISc

August 12, 2022

My attempt to provide a history of queer life in IISc and the movement for queer visibility and representation through the lives of several queer IISc alumni and students. Writing this article was a deeply emotinal process, and filled me with gratitude for the all the queer folk who have fought for queer rights and visibility at IISc.
