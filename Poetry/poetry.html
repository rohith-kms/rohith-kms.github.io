Give me your eyes

Give me your eyes.
Let me place them on the spires
Of the castle that contains my heart.


From there, your eyes can look
At the fields surrounding this castle -
Once a lush green pasture of ladybirds, dragonflies, and
Sunflowers that tried to touch the sun.
Where the blades of grass bent and prayed
Under the morning fog’s gentle hug;
Glistening with a with a light that betrayed
The universe within their transparent dewdrop jewelry.


It may be hard to believe this yellow field was once full
Of dewdrop microcosms with a million lives,
For they were soaked up by the sands of time.
The chlorophyll green grasses are now sunburnt,
Stiff, straw yellow brittle sticks yearning
To prostrate before the sun once more.
Brown crickets drove away the ladybirds
As they lost their diamond dewdrops
Forcing the rainbows to marinate in a sepia hue.


The lush soil is now crusty and parched,
Its moisture evaporated into clouds.
Perhaps somewhere far away, the sky is crying
And the dewdrops joined the ocean salt -
Now denizens of Poseidon’s kingdom, its bricks
Made of tears from countless fields like mine.


Give me your eyes so I can take them
To the towers of the castle around my heart.
From there, let me hold up your eyeballs, and in their gaze,
Refract to a point focus, the afternoon sun’s rays
Somewhere on this endless yellow field to ignite
A fire to complete what the sun left half done.
Let the eagles in my sky be fed with the crickets
That jump to flee the fire’s searing heat.


Let the inferno lions of elemental fire
Wear these licks of flame as their manes,
Join forces with the tornado eagles, and
Burn my yellow field to the ground.
Release the blades of grass from their yearning,
In a spiral descent into their fiery graves –
Let them bend in a last prayer to the sun.


Let my yellow field turn charcoal black.
Let the dormant seeds buried in the ashes
Sprout green phoenix feathers that sway
In the breeze that the sated eagles leave behind.
Let them call out to ladybirds and dragonflies,
And flirt with the clouds, once again.
Let sunflowers bloom and reach for the sun,
And daffodils puff, once again.
Give me your eyes so my fields may burn,
And be alive, once again.
