Hello! I'm Rohith K.M.S.

I’m a Materials Science and Engineering (MSE) graduate from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), specializing in computational modeling and simulation. In my undergraduate thesis project, I simulated the solidification of steel with the aim of making accurate predictions about the spatial variations in the composition of 3D-printed steel parts.

I feel that simulations have tremendous potential as educational tools, and communicating the joy and wonder of science to audiences of all ages and backgrounds is something that resonates with my heart. I also want to make 3D printing accessible, cheap and sustainable, so that all around the world, people have a quick, easy and reliable way to shape their ideas and designs into reality.

Since graduating, I’ve worked as a physics teacher, science writer, and a curator at a science museum. I identify as non-binary and go by the pronouns he/they.

Research

Inter-dendritic Segregation of Austenitic Stainless Steels. (Bachelor's thesis)

2020

Supervisor: Prof. Abhik Choudhury, IISc. Investigated the inter-dendritic segregation in the Iron-Chromium binary system through mesoscale simulations using the phase field method.

Projects

Growth Curve and Optical Density Device (GCODe)

2017

Designed and developed (along with a few others) a device to automate the regular measurement of the optical density of bacterial populations in a culture to generate growth curves, thereby saving biologists countless hours in the lab at a fraction of the price of a commercial automated machine. Open source hardware with detailed DIY instructions. Device nominated for the best hardware prize at the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) competition, 2017.

aNANt functional materials database

2017

Lead frontend and backend developer of aNANt (version 1) - a database (first of its kind in India) of over 23,000 computationally calculated functional materials and their crystal structure, electronic band structure and properties.
